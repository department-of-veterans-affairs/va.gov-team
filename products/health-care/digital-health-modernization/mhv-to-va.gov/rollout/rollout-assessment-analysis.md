### 2. **Analysis & Priorities**

#### **Context**
- All respondents found each item relevant (no "N/A" chosen).
- No one answered "Strongly Disagree," indicating no areas of extreme dysfunction.

#### **Key Areas to Prioritize (Based on Neutral/Negative Responses):**

1. **Official Schedule & Rollout Info**
   - Only 50% agree; 38% neutral, 12% disagree.
   - **Action:** Publish and highlight a single source of schedule truth.

2. **Stakeholder Identification & Communication**
   - Several ‚ÄúNeither agree nor disagree‚Äù and suggestions for notification clarity.
   - **Action:** Map stakeholders, clarify who receives what, when, and how.

3. **SME Review Steps**
   - Multiple neutral/disagree; calls for better criteria.
   - **Action:** Document triggers and process for SME review.

4. **What Users See & Support Data Completeness**
   - High neutral/disagree regarding user-view and support ticket info.
   - **Action:** Ensure documentation/workflows provide precise user-state info.

5. **Team Roles & Communication Pathways**
   - Several mentions of team member understanding and role confusion.
   - **Action:** Document roles, appoint a communication lead, clarify cross-team support.

#### **Additional Themes**
- Desire for more frequent and structured all-team syncs, especially for major initiatives.
- Suggestions to streamline and centralize communications (a ‚Äúsingle point person‚Äù or clear channel ownership).

#### **Recommendations**
- **Short-Term:** 
  - Create a schedule repository/document.
  - Define notifications matrix for stakeholders.
  - Set up regular syncs.

- **Medium-Term:** 
  - Training on SME review triggers and support ticket standards.
  - Document user-data mapping for engineers/support.

- **Long-Term:** 
  - Continually review and iterate communication practices using future surveys.

---

**Summary:**  
No severe breakdowns, but several areas show signs of ambiguity or partial knowledge‚Äîespecially around scheduling, stakeholder communication, SME review processes, and clarity on what users should see. Addressing these gaps will foster better alignment, smoother rollouts, and a more comfortable/supportive team environment.
