## Purpose
The purpose of MHV Accessibility is to support product teams in meeting accessibility requirements and delivering an accessible and usable experience of MyHealtheVet.

## Goals and Objectives
MHV Accessibility program objectives are to advance the following goals:

- MyHealtheVet does not have accessibility barriers.
- Disabled Veterans and their families and caregivers are able to accomplish tasks independently and effectively.
- Product teams have the support needed to meet their accessibility responsibilities.
- Digital accessibility capacity grows as teams and contributors learn and build skills to meet their accessibility responsibilities.
- Accessibility tasks and resources are operationalized, and integrated throughout the development lifecycle.
- Teams and contributors can make accessibility decisions and complete accessibility tasks independently, in real-time.
- Other related goals are bolstered by team outcomes and success measures.

## Target Audience
The audiences that MHV Accessibility aims to support are the following:

- **Users:** Disabled Veterans and their families and caregivers who have accessibility needs
- **Product teams:** Appointments, Medical Records, Medications, Secure Messaging
- **Practice leads:** Horizon, Shared Support
- **Program leadership:** OCTO
- **Program partners:**
  - VA Platform Governance Team
  - VA Accessibility Digital Experience (ADE)
  - VA Section 508 Office
- **Support:** Customer Support and Triage

## Success Measures
MHV Accessibility will measure and monitor the following measures in order to track progress toward meeting goals and objectives:

### Primary

- **Proactive practices:** Number of accessibility issues flagged during accessibility reviews by Governance Team and 508 Office (low number indicates accessibility requirements are addressed in product development)
- **Defect mitigation:** Number of known (open) accessibility tickets; severity of issues in open accessibility tickets (high number indicates likelihood of accessibility issues experienced by end-users)
- **Improved user experience:** Number of accessibility issues reported by end-users (low number indicates accessibility requirements are addressed in product development)

### Aspirational

- **Inclusive research:** Number of research studies with AT participants; number of AT users (high indicates representation of disabled perspectives in research data) 
- **Proactive practices:** Number of Github tickets and tasks with accessibility requirements and user stories (high number indicates accessibility requirements are addressed in product development)
