## Background

This study evaluates design directions for allowing Veterans to file Supplemental Claims in the online 526EZ application.

Two [prototypes](https://www.figma.com/proto/P6IUm8gN00H3Yi1XzN80fj/-526ez--Condition-information-entry?page-id=6174%3A18589&node-id=6524-47192&viewport=653%2C-7741%2C0.75&t=lVGxess9idiVg78M-1&scaling=min-zoom&content-scaling=fixed&starting-point-node-id=6524%3A47192&show-proto-sidebar=1) were tested: â€œAppleâ€ and â€œBanana.â€ There were two key differences between the prototypes:
- Display of denied conditions
  - â€œAppleâ€ includes a Veteranâ€™s denied conditions on the Add a condition screen, along with their rated disabilities and the option to add a new condition
  - â€œBananaâ€ puts denied conditions on a secondary page. It also has a slightly different visual treatment for Add a condition options
- Options for claiming rated disabilities
  - If a Veteran adds a rated disability to their claim, Apple assumes they want to file a Claim for Increase
  - Banana, on the other hand, requires Veterans to choose between a Claim for Increase and a Supplemental Claim on a rated disability

## Study goals
The primary goal of this study was to determine which of the designs best meets user needs when filing a supplemental claim within the online 526, without negatively impacting the user experience of filing a standard disability claim. This top-line summary focuses on this goal.

Secondary study goals will be addressed in the full research report.
