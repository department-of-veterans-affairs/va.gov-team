# Veteran Status Card 

## New Home & User Experience Product Outline


### Overview

The IIR team is implementing a new way for Veterans to digitally access and use their Veteran Status Card both on the web and the mobile app. The work will also include rehoming the Veteran Status on va.gov within profile. 


### Problem Statement(s)

- The web and mobile app experiences are not aligned (they have different homes in each experience and look very different)

- The current experience is not meeting OCTO/government goals of having digital experiences that are “consistent with \[the public’s] favorite consumer websites and mobile apps.”

- It is not easy to use (Veterans have to download and/or print it on va.gov)

- It is not an industry standard experience (think airline tickets, event tickets, insurance cards, etc.)

How might we statement: How might we ease the burden of Veterans accessing discounts and memberships through a digital experience that requires fewer steps to gain access to the card, store the card, and use the card.


### Desired User Outcomes

- Veterans can easily find their Veteran Status Card (rehoming in Profile)

- Veterans can easily access their Veteran Status Card (redesigning the card and page)

- Veterans can easily use their Veteran Status Card (adding mobile wallet capabilities)

- Veterans experience similar user flows and designs between web and mobile app.


### Undesired User Outcomes

- Veterans are not able to easily find their Veteran Status Card

- Veterans are not able to easily access their Veteran Status Card

- Veterans are not able to easily use their Veteran Status Card 

- Veterans have different experiences across platforms resulting in confusion and frustration.


### Desired Business Outcomes

- Successful pilot for a team building an experience across web and mobile.

- Veteran Status Card becomes the primary ID card (allowing for the potential decommission of the Veteran ID Card (VIC)).

- Increase the number of Veterans that view and use their Veteran Status Card.
